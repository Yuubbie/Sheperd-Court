About the Book

In the small coastal town of Ravenwood Bay, the discovery of human remains buried beneath the old lighthouse sends shockwaves through the tight-knit community. For Detective Claire Morgan, newly returned to her hometown after a decade away, the case quickly becomes personal when evidence links the decades-old murder to her own family's past.

As Claire delves deeper into the investigation, she uncovers a web of secrets that the town's most prominent families have kept hidden for generations. With each revelation, the list of suspects grows, including her childhood friends, a reclusive artist with a mysterious past, and even her own estranged father.

When another body is discovered and it becomes clear that the killer is still active, Claire must race against time to piece together the fragments of truth before more lives are lost. But in a town where everyone has something to hide, distinguishing allies from enemies may prove impossible.

"The Silent Echo" is a gripping mystery that explores the dangerous power of secrets, the weight of family legacies, and the echoes of past sins that reverberate into the present.

Chapter 1: Return to Ravenwood

The lighthouse stood sentinel on the cliff's edge, its weathered white exterior gleaming in the late afternoon sun. Claire Morgan pulled her car to a stop at the gravel lot and sat for a moment, taking in the view she had deliberately avoided for ten years. The rhythmic crash of waves against the rocks below provided a familiar soundtrack that transported her back to her childhood.

She hadn't planned to return to Ravenwood Bay. Not after everything that had happened. But when the police chief—her godfather and her father's former partner—had called with the job offer, circumstances had made the decision for her.

"It's time to come home, Claire," Tom had said, his gravelly voice softened by concern. "The department needs someone with your experience. And maybe... maybe you need this too."

She stepped out of the car, the cool September breeze carrying the scent of salt and pine. Her boots crunched on the gravel as she approached the metal gate that blocked the path to the lighthouse. The faded "No Trespassing" sign hung limply from a single nail, swaying slightly in the wind.

This wasn't an official visit. She didn't start as detective until Monday. But something had drawn her here before reporting for duty. Perhaps a need to face the ghosts of her past before diving into her new role.

Her phone buzzed in her pocket. Tom's name flashed on the screen.

"Claire," he said without preamble when she answered. "Where are you?"

"Just getting reacquainted with the town. I'm at the lighthouse."

A pause. Too long to be casual.

"You need to come to the station. Now."

The tension in his voice sent a chill through her that had nothing to do with the coastal breeze.

"What's happened?"

"Construction crew was breaking ground for that new visitor center by the lighthouse this morning." His voice was tight, controlled. "They found something. Human remains, Claire. Looks like they've been there for decades."

Claire's gaze drifted back to the lighthouse, its beam not yet illuminated in the dimming afternoon light. For generations, it had guided ships safely to shore, warning them away from the treacherous rocks. But it seemed the lighthouse had been keeping secrets too—dark secrets buried in its shadow.

"I'm on my way."

As she pulled away, Claire couldn't shake the feeling that the lighthouse was watching her, its dark windows like eyes following her departure. She had returned to Ravenwood Bay to escape her past, only to find herself immediately confronted with the town's own buried history.

And somehow, she knew, the two were connected.

About the Author

Jessica Winters

Mystery & Thriller Author

Jessica Winters is the bestselling author of the Detective Claire Morgan series and numerous standalone thrillers. Before turning to writing full-time, she worked as a criminal psychologist, an experience that informs the psychological depth of her characters and the intricate mysteries she crafts.

Born and raised in a small coastal town similar to those featured in her novels, Jessica draws inspiration from the hidden histories and complex relationships that exist in close-knit communities. Her work has been praised for its atmospheric settings, sharp dialogue, and surprising twists.

When not writing, Jessica enjoys hiking along the Pacific coast, collecting vintage mystery novels, and volunteering with literacy programs in underserved communities. She lives in Portland, Oregon with her husband and two rescue dogs.
