Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about our publishing services

How long does the publishing process take?

The timeframe varies depending on the complexity of your project and the services required. On average, the complete process from manuscript submission to publication takes 3-6 months. This includes editing, design, formatting, and distribution setup. We'll provide you with a detailed timeline during your initial consultation.

Do I retain the rights to my book?

Yes, absolutely. At Shepherd Court Publishing, you retain 100% of the rights to your work. We believe authors should maintain full ownership and creative control of their intellectual property. Our role is to help you publish and distribute your work professionally, not to own it.

What is the difference between traditional and self-publishing?

In traditional publishing, a publisher purchases the rights to your manuscript, pays you an advance, handles all aspects of production, and pays royalties on sales. The author has limited control over the process.

With self-publishing, you maintain complete control and receive higher royalties, but you're responsible for all aspects of production and marketing. Our services provide the professional support of traditional publishing while allowing you to maintain the control and higher royalties of self-publishing.

How are royalties calculated and paid?

You receive 100% of your net royalties from all sales. Payments are made quarterly, and you'll have access to detailed sales reporting through our author portal. The exact royalty percentage varies by distribution channel and format, but typically ranges from 60-85% of net proceeds. We'll provide a detailed breakdown in your publishing agreement.

Can I make changes to my book after it's published?

Yes, one of the advantages of our publishing model is the ability to make updates to your book. Whether you need to correct errors or release an updated edition, we can help implement these changes. There may be a small fee for extensive revisions, but minor corrections are often covered in your publishing package.
